The one-line diagnosis

Creative, offer, and page are all healthy. People click, land, add to cart, and reach checkout — then 0 purchases (7 checkouts, 0 sales). The money is dying at checkout + tracking, not at the ad or the offer. Fix the purchase step before spending more on traffic.

0 Tracking & Checkout CRITICAL — DO FIRST

This gates everything below. Nothing converts until a purchase can register.

  • Run a US test order end-to-end — use a US VPN and a real US shipping address. A VPN only shows the US experience; the real test is completing payment.
  • Confirm the Purchase event fires in Meta Events Manager (check CAPI deduplication). ATC + InitiateCheckout already fire — Purchase is the gap.
  • Verify free shipping shows on 2+ packs for US and there are no payment errors.
  • Consolidate to one pixel / one domain. Spreading across get.lunanaturals.co, getlunanatural.co, and lunanaturals.co fragments tracking and trust.
Best case: have an actual US-based person place and then refund a real order while you watch Events Manager live.

Lead with mosquitoes + kids; keep ticks as a secondary support line, not the headline.

Why soften the aggressive claims

Trust is the bottleneck (0 purchases) — these claims attack it:
  • "Save 60%" → on an unknown brand it reads "was it ever worth full price? is this cheap junk?" Steep discounts signal desperation and cheapen a kids' health product.
  • Disneyland giveaway → feels like a sweepstakes / data-grab funnel, not a health brand. Distracts from safety and reads "dropshipper."
  • "50,000+ reviews" → not believable for a brand nobody knows. The other page's 824 reviews feels real and converts. Believable beats big.

Keep the credible proof: pediatrician-reviewed, third-party tested, real named testimonials.
